Research

My research integrates photovoltaic device physics, scalable fabrication, advanced characterization, and reliability engineering. The central objective is to translate high-efficiency perovskite devices into reproducible, durable, and manufacturable photovoltaic technologies.

01

Perovskite photovoltaics

Composition and crystallization control for high-efficiency normal and inverted perovskite solar cells, including wide- and low-bandgap absorbers for tandem applications.

02

Scalable processing and vacuum flash

Vacuum-assisted crystallization, blade coating, slot-die coating, pressure-profile engineering, rheology, drying kinetics, in-line optical monitoring, and module-scale process integration.

03

Interfaces and charge transport

SAMs, dopant-free HTMs, SnOx, TiO2, passivation materials, interfacial recombination, charge extraction, and QFLS/PL/EL characterization.

04

Reliability and space photovoltaics

Encapsulation, coupled light–heat–vacuum stresses, radiation effects, defect evolution, and stability protocols for terrestrial and space-relevant photovoltaic deployment.

05

Energy storage materials

Modified carbon-felt electrodes, doped TiO2 nanostructures, selective membranes, and electrochemical diagnostics for vanadium redox flow batteries.
